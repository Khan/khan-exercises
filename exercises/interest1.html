person(1) borrowed $PRINCIPAL from the Bank today, and must repay the loan in one payment, PERIOD yearplural(PERIOD) from now. The loan carries a compound interest rate of INTEREST_RATE% per year, compounded annually. How much money does person(1) need to repay?

FUTURE_VALUE

The amount person(1) needs to pay is calculated by taking the principal amount person(1) borrowed, and compounding it by the interest rate r each year for the duration of the loan, n.

In other words, Future Value = Present Value * (1 + r)^n

Substituting our values, Future Value = PRINCIPAL * (1 + INTEREST_RATE/100 )^ PERIOD

Future Value = FUTURE_VALUE

person(1) borrowed $PRINCIPAL from the Bank today, and must repay the loan in one payment, PERIOD yearplural(PERIOD) from now. It is a simple interest loan with a rate of INTEREST_RATE% per year. How much money does person(1) need to repay?

FUTURE_VALUE_SIMPLE_INTEREST

person(1) needs to repay the principal amount he(1) borrowed, as well as a fixed percentage of the principal amount for each year that he(1) borrowed the money.

The percentage person(1) needs to repay each year is the interest rate r multiplied by the principal amount person(1) borrowed. Adding up all the yearly interest payments to the principal gives us the amount he(1) needs to repay.

Put into a formula, Future Value = Present Value * (1 + t*r)

Substituting our values, Future Value = PRINCIPAL * (1 + PERIOD * INTEREST_RATE/100 )

Future Value = FUTURE_VALUE
