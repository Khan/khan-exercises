Since \tan{(\pi/4)} = 1, the limit can be rewritten as
\displaystyle\lim_{x \to \pi/4} \dfrac{ \tan{x} - \tan{(\pi/4)} }{x-\pi/4}

Now rewrite the tangents in terms of sine and cosine.

Since \tan{x} = \dfrac{\sin{x}}{\cos{x}}, the limit can be rewritten as

\displaystyle\lim_{x \to \pi/4} \dfrac{ \dfrac{\sin{x}}{\cos{x}} - \dfrac{\sin{(\pi/4)}}{\cos{(\pi/4)}} }{x-\pi/4}

Combine the fractions in the numerator and use a trigonometric identity.

Combining the fractions in the numerator of the limit gives
\dfrac{\sin{x}}{\cos{x}} - \dfrac{\sin{(\pi/4)}}{\cos{(\pi/4)}} = \dfrac{\sin{x}\cos{(\pi/4)} - \cos{x}\sin{(\pi/4)}}{\cos{x}\cos{(\pi/4)}}

Using the double angle formula, \sin{(A-B)} = \sin{A}\cos{B} - \cos{A}\sin{B}
the limit can now be written as
\displaystyle\lim_{x\to \pi/4} \dfrac{\sin{(x-\pi/4)}}{x-\pi/4} \dfrac{1}{\cos{x}\cos{\pi/4}}

The Product Rule for limits says \displaystyle\lim_{x\to p} f(x)g(x) = \lim_{x\to p}f(x) \lim_{x\to p} g(x),
so \displaystyle\lim_{x\to \pi/4} \dfrac{\sin{(x-\pi/4)}}{x-\pi/4} \dfrac{1}{\cos{x}\cos{\pi/4}} = \displaystyle\lim_{x\to \pi/4} \dfrac{\sin{(x-\pi/4)}}{x-\pi/4} \lim_{x\to \pi/4}\dfrac{1}{\cos{x}\cos{(\pi/4)}}

By making the substitution, y=x-\pi/4, in the first limit of the product, we get
\displaystyle\lim_{x\to \pi/4} \dfrac{\sin{(x-\pi/4)}}{x-\pi/4} = \lim_{y\to 0} \dfrac{\sin{y}}{y} = 1.
The second limit of the product evaluates to 2 since
\displaystyle\lim_{x\to \pi/4}\dfrac{1}{\cos{x}\cos{(\pi/4)}} = \dfrac{1}{\cos{(\pi/4)}\cos{(\pi/4)}} = \dfrac{1}{[\cos{(\pi/4)}]^2} = \dfrac{1}{ (\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}})^2 } = \dfrac{1}{1/2} = 2.

Putting all of this together we get \displaystyle\lim_{x \to \pi/4} \dfrac{ \tan{x} - 1 }{x-\pi/4} = 2.
