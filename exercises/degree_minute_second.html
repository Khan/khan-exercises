Conversion from Decimal Degrees to Degrees Minutes Seconds uses the Modulo operation in Math.
The Modulo function keeps track of the remainder of some division, in this case division by 1.
When you "take the remainder" of some division you are using the modulo function.

The Whole Number DEG Degrees remain the same and you multiply the decimal portion roundTo(6,DECDEG%1) by (\frac{60 min}{1 degree}) to get "decimal" minutes.

roundTo(6,DECDEG%1) * (\frac{60 min}{1 degree}) = roundTo(6,(DECDEG%1)*60) minutes

Now the take the value for decimal minutes roundTo(6,(DECDEG%1)*60) and repeat the operation with the decimal portion to get decimal seconds.

roundTo(6,(((DECDEG%1)*60)%1)) * (\frac{60 sec}{1 minute}) = roundTo(6,(((DECDEG%1)*60)%1)*60) seconds

Rounding to the hundreths place for seconds gives you the final answer of

DEG degrees MIN minutes SECCOMPLT seconds.

For reference 0.01 seconds of latitude is equal to about 30 cm or 1 foot in distance.
