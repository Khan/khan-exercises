Express A as a fraction.

A / 1

(You can express this as any fraction - you don't have to simplify your answer)

The number A * 10 is in the the tenths place.

Since there is one decimal place, we simply put the number over 10.

The answer is fraction( A * 10, 10 ).

Express B as a fraction.

B / 1

The number ( floor( B * 10 )).toFixed(0) is in the the tenths place.

The number ( floor( B * 100 )).toFixed(0) - ( floor( B * 10 )).toFixed(0) * 10 is in the the hundredths place.

This then becomes fraction(( floor( B * 10 )).toFixed(0), 10 ) + fraction(( floor( B * 100 )).toFixed(0) - ( floor( B * 10 )).toFixed(0) * 10 , 100 )

Finding a common denominator: fraction(( floor( B * 10 )).toFixed(0) * 10, 100 ) + fraction(( floor( B * 100 )).toFixed(0) - ( floor( B * 10 )).toFixed(0) * 10 , 100 )

Or more simply: Since there are two decimal places, we simply put the number over 100.

The answer is fraction( roundTo( 2, B * 100 ) , 100 ).
