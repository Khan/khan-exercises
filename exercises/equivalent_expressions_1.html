Fill in the missing term to make the expressions equivalent.

C * (D + E) = C*D + \mbox{?}

C*E

Remember the distributive law: the number outside the parentheses gets distributed over the addition within the parentheses.

Begin by distributing C over (D + E), C*(D+E) = C*D + C*E.

Simplify the resulting expression, C*D + C*E = C*D + C*E so the answer is C*E.
