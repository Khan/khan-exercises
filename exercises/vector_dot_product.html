What is the dot product \boldsymbol{a}\cdot\boldsymbol{b} of the vectors \boldsymbol{a}=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}A1 \\ A2\\A3 \\ A4\end{array}\right] and \boldsymbol{b}=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}B1 \\ B2\\B3 \\ B4\end{array}\right]?

SOLUTION

The dot product of two vectors is the product of the first two entries of each vector plus the product of the second two entries of each vector and so on.

You may have seen dot products without realizing it: for instance, you can write the polynomial 2x+3y=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\cdot\left[\begin{array}{cccc}x \\ y \end{array}\right]. Similarly, and the formula for distance from the origin to the point (x,y) is the square root of x^2+y^2=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}x \\ y \end{array}\right]\cdot\left[\begin{array}{cccc}x \\ y \end{array}\right]

The formula for the dot product of two vectors with four components is:

\boldsymbol{a}\cdot\boldsymbol{b}= \left[\begin{array}{cccc}\color{orange}{a_1} \\ \color{blue}{a_2} \\ \color{red}{a_3} \\ \color{green}{a_4}\end{array}\right]\cdot \left[\begin{array}{cccc}\color{orange}{b_1} \\ \color{blue}{b_2} \\ \color{red}{b_3} \\ \color{green}{b_4}\end{array}\right]= \color{orange}{a_1 * b_1} + \color{blue}{a_2 * b_2} + \color{red}{a_3 * b_3} + \color{green}{a_4 * b_4}.

Plugging the values for \boldsymbol{a} and \boldsymbol{b} into the formula, we get:

\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\color{orange}{A1} \\ \color{blue}{A2} \\ \color{red}{A3} \\ \color{green}{A4}\end{array}\right]\cdot \left[\begin{array}{cccc}\color{orange}{B1} \\ \color{blue}{B2} \\ \color{red}{B3} \\ \color{green}{B4}\end{array}\right]= \color{orange}{A1 * B1} + \color{blue}{A2 * B2} + \color{red}{A3 * B3} + \color{green}{A4 * B4}

Calculating the right hand side, we get the answer:
