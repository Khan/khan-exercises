Anything to the first power is the number itself.

i ^ 1 = i

The most important property of the imaginary unit i is that i ^ 2 = -1.

i ^ 3 = (i ^ 2) \cdot i = (-1) cdot i = -i

i ^ 4 = (i ^ 2) ^ 2 = (-1) ^ 2 = 1

i ^ EXP = SOLUTION

4 + randRange(1, 30) (function() { switch (EXP % 4) { case 0: return '1'; case 1: return 'i'; case 2: return '-1'; case 3: return '-i'; } })()

Simplify.

i ^ {EXP}

SOLUTION

  • 1
  • i
  • -1
  • -i

The most important property of the imaginary unit i is that i ^ 2 = -1.

When this property is plugged into i ^ 4, we get: i ^ 4 = (i ^ 2) ^ 2 = (-1) ^ 2 = 1

Plugging this result into i ^ {EXP}, we get: i ^ {EXP} = i ^ 4 \cdot i ^ {EXP - 4} = i ^ {EXP - 4}

Whenever we want to find i ^ X with X \geq 4, we can drop 4 off X.

The number that remains when we decrease a number by 4 while it's greater than 4 is the remainder after dividing the number by 4.

The remainder after dividing EXP by 4 is EXP % 4, so i ^ {EXP} = i ^ {EXP % 4}.

i ^ {EXP} = i ^ {EXP % 4} = SOLUTION.
