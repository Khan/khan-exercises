\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{\text{$._("percent")}}}{100} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{$._("part")}}}{\color{GREEN}{\text{$._("whole")}}}

The WHOLE is the \color{GREEN}{\text{$._("whole")}}. We are trying to find the \color{PINK}{\text{$._("part")}} that makes up PART_PERCENT\% of it:

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{PART_PERCENT}}{100} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{$._("part")}}}{\color{GREEN}{WHOLE}}

If we multiply the denominator of the fraction on the left by MULTIPLE, it will be the same denominator of the fraction on the right. To keep things equal, let's also multiply the numerator on the left by MULTIPLE:

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{PART_PERCENT} \times MULTIPLE}{100 \times MULTIPLE} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{$._("part")}}}{\color{GREEN}{WHOLE}}

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{PART_PERCENT * MULTIPLE}}{WHOLE} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{$._("part")}}}{\color{GREEN}{WHOLE}}

\qquad \color{BLUE}{PART_PERCENT * MULTIPLE} = \color{PINK}{\text{$._("part")}}
