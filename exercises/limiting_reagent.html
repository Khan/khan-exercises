Find the limiting reagent in the following chemical reaction, if mass_a grams of A and mass_b grams of B are present in the reaction vessel before the initiation of the reaction and the atomic masses of A and B are atomic_mass_a g/mol and atomic_mass_b g/mol respectively.

reagent_aA + reagent_bB => A_reagent_aB_reagent_b

The limiting reagent is the substance that is totally consumed when the reaction is complete.

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of the reactants.

Number of Moles of A in the reaction vessel is moles_of_a

Number of Moles of B in the reaction vessel is moles_of_b

Step 2: Divide moles of each reagent by its stoichiometric coefficient.

A : moles_of_a/reagent_a = moles_of_a/reagent_a

B : moles_of_b/reagent_b = moles_of_b/reagent_b

Step 3: Identify the limiting reagent.

As both the values are equal, we can conclude that both the reagents are consumed fully.
