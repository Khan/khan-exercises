Express the improper fraction as a decimal:

\dfrac{IMPROPER_NUMERATOR}{DENOMINATOR}

IMPROPER_NUMERATOR / DENOMINATOR

An improper fraction in which the numerator is larger than the denominator.

\frac{\color{GREEN}{IMPROPER_NUMERATOR}}{\color{BLUE}{DENOMINATOR}} is an improper fraction because the numerator, IMPROPER_NUMERATOR, is larger than the denominator, DENOMINATOR.

The fraction \frac{\color{GREEN}{IMPROPER_NUMERATOR}}{\color{BLUE}{DENOMINATOR}} is equivalent to dividing the numerator by the denominator.

\begin{eqnarray} \dfrac{\color{GREEN}{IMPROPER_NUMERATOR}}{\color{BLUE}{DENOMINATOR}} & \qquad = \qquad & \color{GREEN}{IMPROPER_NUMERATOR} \div \color{BLUE}{DENOMINATOR} \end{eqnarray}

To convert this fraction to a decimal, divide IMPROPER_NUMERATOR by DENOMINATOR. If you need help with division, see Sal's video, Converting Fractions to Decimals.

Dividing IMPROPER_NUMERATOR by DENOMINATOR we see that \dfrac{\color{GREEN}{IMPROPER_NUMERATOR}}{\color{BLUE}{DENOMINATOR}} = \color{ORANGE}{DECIMAL}. Whenever the numerator is larger than the denominator, (that is, whenever you have an improper fraction) the decimal will be larger than 1. In this case, the decimal is larger than \color{ORANGE}{parseInt( ( DECIMAL ), 10)}.

\color{ORANGE}{DECIMAL} is the value of our fraction as expressed as a decimal, also known as a decimal fraction.
