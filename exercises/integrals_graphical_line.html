The equation MX x + NY y = C is a line so, geometrically speaking, the area under it can be divided into right triangles and possibly a rectangle.

drawArea = true; graph.update();

The integral we want represents the area under the curve, so all we need to do to find the areas of these shapes.

To find the dimensions of the shapes, begin by solving for y: y = f(x) = SLOPEx + YINTERCEPT.

The widths of our shapes can be determined from the bounds of the integral a = A and b = B (and possibly the x-intercept if the line crosses the x-axis within these bounds). The heighs are determined by the values of f(a) = FA and f(b) = FB. You can now calculate the area under the curve.

Don't forget: Area under the x-axis counts as negative.

In conclusion, \int_{A}^{B}f(x)dx = ANS.
