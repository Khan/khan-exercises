person(1) borrowed $commafy(PRINCIPAL) from the Bank today, and must repay the loan in one payment, PERIOD yearplural(PERIOD) from now. The loan carries a compound interest rate of INTEREST_RATE% per year, compounded annually. How much money does person(1) need to repay?

FUTURE_VALUE

The amount person(1) needs to pay is calculated by taking the principal amount person(1) borrowed, and compounding it by the interest rate r each year for the duration of the loan, n.

We can calculate the amount for each year by multiplying the amount for that year with the loan's interest rate:
Future Value (year 1) = Principal * (1 + rate)
Future Value (year 2) = Future Value(year 1) * (1 + rate)
and so on...

This is more easily expressed by the formula:
Future Value = Present Value * (1 + r)^n

Substituting our values, Future Value = PRINCIPAL * (1 + INTEREST_RATE/100 )^ PERIOD

Future Value = $commafy(FUTURE_VALUE)

person(1) borrowed $PRINCIPAL from the Bank today, and must repay the loan in one payment, PERIOD yearplural(PERIOD) from now. It is a simple interest loan with a rate of INTEREST_RATE\% per year. How much money will person(1) need to repay?

FUTURE_VALUE_SIMPLE_INTEREST

person(1) needs to repay the principal amount he(1) borrowed, as well as a fixed percentage of the principal amount for each year that he(1) borrowed the money.

The percentage person(1) needs to repay for each year the loan is held is the interest rate r multiplied by the principal amount person(1) borrowed. Adding up all the yearly interest payments to the principal gives us the amount he(1) needs to repay.

This means that each yearly interest is:
Yearly Interest = Principal * rate = PRINCIPAL*INTEREST_RATE/100 = PRINCIPAL * (INTEREST_RATE/100)

So, the total amount to pay back is the principal plus PERIOD yearly payments:
Future Value = Principal + t * YearlyPayment = PRINCIPAL + PERIOD * PRINCIPAL * (INTEREST_RATE/100) = FUTURE_VALUE_SIMPLE_INTEREST

Another way to do this is to use this simplified formula:
Future Value = Principal * (1 + t*r)

Substituting our values, Future Value = PRINCIPAL * (1 + PERIOD * INTEREST_RATE/100 )

Future Value = $commafy(FUTURE_VALUE_SIMPLE_INTEREST)
