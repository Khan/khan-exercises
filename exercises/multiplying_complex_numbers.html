(A_REP) \cdot (B_REP)

the separate parts of a complex number (5 + 3i has real part 5, imaginary part 3)

Complex numbers are multiplied like any two binomials.

First use the distributive property:
\qquad (A_REP) \cdot (B_REP) = (A_REAL \cdot B_REAL) + (A_REAL \cdot B_IMAGi) + (A_IMAGi \cdot B_REAL) + (A_IMAGi \cdot B_IMAGi)

Then simplify the terms:
\qquad (A_REAL * B_REAL) + (A_REAL * B_IMAGi) + (A_IMAG * B_REALi) + (A_IMAG * B_IMAG \cdot i^2)

Imaginary unit multiples can be grouped together.
\qquad (A_REAL * B_REAL) + (A_REAL * B_IMAG + A_IMAG * B_REAL)i + (A_IMAG * B_IMAGi^2)

After we plug in i^2 = -1, the result becomes (A_REAL * B_REAL) + (A_REAL * B_IMAG + A_IMAG * B_REAL)i - (A_IMAG * B_IMAG)

The result is simplified: (A_REAL * B_REAL - A_IMAG * B_IMAG) + (ANSWER_IMAGi) = ANSWER_REAL + ANSWER_IMAGi

The real part of the result is ANSWER_REAL and the imaginary part is ANSWER_IMAG.
