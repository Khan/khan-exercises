Playing a game with available pegs, all of them unique, you can put choose of them in the order of your choice. How many permutations are available to you?

permutations

The number of permutations of available, choosing choose, when order matters, is a function of the factorial of both numbers.

If you have an orange, blue, and green peg, OBG, and can choose two of them, they can be arranged as OB, OG, OB, BG, GB, or GO.

The number of permutations of N distinct objects, choosing choose, is PERMUTATIONS when order matters.
