Playing a game with avail pegs (toSentence(all_colors, 'and')), you can put pick of them into slots the order of your choice. How many permutations are available to you?

nperm

You can choose any of the avail colors to fill the only slot available.

You can choose any of the avail colors to fill the first slot. Since you cannot use the color again, you only have avail - 1 choices available for the second slot, and so forth. You need to multiply together avail, avail - 1, etc, until you run out of slots.

You only multiply by numbers you have slots for. You can see this as having avail!, and dividing by the (avail-pick)! that you don't have room for.

Here's another way to see it. Select pick pegs, for instance toSentence(peg_colors, 'and'). For example, if you choose the colors toSentence(peg_colors, 'and'), they can be permuted in perms.length ways, describe_permutations(perms). You can choose nperm/pick different combinations of colors.

perms.length*nperm/pick = nperm.
