Coordinates are listed as (\color{blue}{x},\color{green}{y}).

So, for ( \color{blue}{POINT[0]}, \color{green}{POINT[1]} ) our \color{blue}{x}-coordinate is \color{blue}{POINT[0]} and our \color{green}{y}-coordinate is \color{green}{POINT[1]}.

The \color{blue}{x}-coordinate tells how far we move to the right from the origin and the \color{green}{y}-coordinate tells us how far we move up from the origin.

Since our \color{blue}{x}-coordinate is equal to \color{blue}{POINT[0]}, we don't need to move to the right.

Since our \color{blue}{x}-coordinate is equal to \color{blue}{POINT[0]}, we move \color{blue}{POINT[0]} to the right.

Since our \color{green}{y}-coordinate is \color{green}{POINT[1]}, we don't need to move up.
