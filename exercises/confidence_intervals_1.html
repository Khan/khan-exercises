If you can figure out the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the mean ({\Large{\sigma{}_{\bar{x}}}}), you can figure out how many standard deviations away your bounds are from your sample mean, and look up the value in the z-scores table.

Since we have sampled a lot of FRUITS, the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the mean ({\Large{\sigma{}_{\bar{x}}}}) can be approximated as {\LARGE{\frac{\sigma{}}{\sqrt{n}}}}, or {\LARGE{\frac{SAMPLE_STD_DEV}{\sqrt{SAMPLE_SIZE}}}}

If {\Large{\sigma{}_{\bar{x}}}} is SAMPLE_STD_DEV/SAMPLE_SIZE_SQRT, then our sample's mean of SAMPLE_MEAN and our upper bound of UPPER_BOUND are STD_DEV_DISTANCE standard deviations apart. We can use this value and the z-score table to find our answer.

Don't forget that the z-scores table values include the entire left tail of the normal distribution, which doesn't account for the lower bound given in the problem.
