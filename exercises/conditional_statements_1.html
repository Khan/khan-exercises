The hypothesis is the phrase that follows the "if" and the conclusion is the phrase that follows the "then".

"If" implies the condition, it is not part of the condition itself. "Then" implies the conclusion, it is not part of the conclusion itself.

Sometimes "if-then" statements are written in the reverse order with the conclusion written first.
