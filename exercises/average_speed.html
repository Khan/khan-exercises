A car travels a path with average speed equals to SPEED_A m/s.

It goes back through the same path with average speed equals to SPEED_B m/s.

What is the total average speed of the car?

2/( 1/SPEED_A + 1/SPEED_B )

Remember that average speed is equals to distance over time, i.e. r= \dfrac{d}{t}.

The distance traveled by the car is the same in both directions.

Let r_{total} be the total average speed, d the distance that the car transversing the path, and t_1 and t_2 the time that it takes to go and come back through this path, respectively. Then r_{total} = \dfrac{2d}{t_1 + t_2}.

The average speed of the car when it is going is equal to SPEED_A =\dfrac{d}{t_1}. Thus, the time that it takes to transverse the path going is equal to t_1 = \dfrac{d}{SPEED_A} .

Analogously, the time that the car takes when it is coming back is equal to t_2 = \dfrac{d}{SPEED_B}. Therefore r_{total} = \dfrac{2d}{ \dfrac{d}{ SPEED_A} + \dfrac{d}{SPEED_B } } \Leftrightarrow r_{total} = \dfrac{2}{ \dfrac{1}{SPEED_A} + \dfrac{1}{SPEED_B} }. r_{total} = roundTo( 1 , 2/(1/SPEED_A + 1/SPEED_B) ).
