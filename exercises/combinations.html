What is the relationship between permutations and combinations?

With a permutation, order matters. With a combination, order doesn't matter. This means that there are many permutations for each combination. For example, the combination (A, B, C) has are six permutations: (A, B, C), (A, C, B), (B, A, C), (B, C, A), (C, A, B), and (C, B, A).
