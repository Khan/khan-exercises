DET

The determinant of a matrix conveys a great deal of information about it. For instance if \lvert A \rvert\neq 0, then the matrix has an inverse A^{-1}, its columns (or rows) are linearly independent, and the equation A\boldsymbol{x}=\boldsymbol{k} has a unique solution given by \boldsymbol{x}=A^{-1}\boldsymbol{k}.

The determinant of a 2x2 matrix is given by the following formula:

\begin{vmatrix}a & b\\ c & d\end{vmatrix}= a * d - b * c

Using the formula, we get:

\begin{vmatrix}A11 & A12\\A21 & A22\end{vmatrix} = A11 * A22 - A21 * A12
