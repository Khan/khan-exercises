You've been transported to the world of Harry Potter. You are leading a magical expedition with a group of 4 students from Hogwarts.

There are APPLICANTS students applying. Any of the applicants can be chosen in your group.

How many different groups can be formed?

NUM_GROUPS

Hint #1: When you count the different groups, Order does NOT matter! This is a combination problem
The combination formula will calculate the different ways of selecting a group of items when order does not matter. The formula for a combination is:

\dbinom{N}{R} = _NC_R = \dfrac{N!}{R!*(N-R)!}

Hint #2: What is the N in this problem? What is the R in this problem?
N is equal to APPLICANTS student applicants.
R is equal to the 4 people you are choosing. Calculate:

\dbinom{APPLICANTS}{4} = \dfrac{APPLICANTS!}{4!*(APPLICANTS-4)!}

Hint #3: The answer is NUM_GROUPS different groups.
