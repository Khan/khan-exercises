You've been transported to the world of Harry Potter. You are leading a magical expedition with a group of 4 students from Hogwarts.

GRYFF students from the house of Gryffindor, SLYTH from Slytherin, HUFFLE from Hufflepuff, and RAVEN from Ravenclaw applied to join your team. Group members are chosen randomly.

How many different groups can be formed?

ANSWER

Hint #1:
Let's change the problem to make it more simple.

You are leading a group of 2 students from Hogwarts. Pretend Harry and Hermoine from Gryffindor, and Draco from Slytherin are applying. How many different groups can be formed? Let's write out all the possible groups.

  • Harry and Hermoine
  • Harry and Draco
  • Hermoine and Draco

There are 3 different groups you can form. Notice how we did not count both Harry and Hermione and Hermione and Harry. Is there a technique that can help you count the different groups when you don't care about order?

Hint #2:
The combination formula will calculate the different ways of selecting a group of things when order does not matter. The formula for a combination is:

nCr = n! / r!(n-r)!

In our simple problem from Hint #1, the n is 3 and the r is 2. There are 3 people applying and you want to grab 2 people as part of your group.
How can we apply that same technique to the original problem now?

Hint #3:
We need to count the total number of people applying to be our n.
This is GRYFF + SLYTH + HUFFLE + RAVEN.
There are APPLICANTS people applying and you want to grab 4 people so the answer is (APPLICANTS choose 4).
