What is \vec a \cdot \vec b?

\begin{align*} \vec a &= AX \hat\imath + AY \hat\jmath \\ \vec b &= BX \hat\imath + BY \hat\jmath \end{align*}

AX * BX + AY * BY

The dot product of two vectors is defined as the sum of the products of the vectors' individual components.

Multiply the \hat\imath and \hat\jmath components separately, then add them up.

\vec a \cdot \vec b = (AX\cdotBX) + (AY\cdotBY)

= AX * BX + AY * BY

= AX * BX + AY * BY
