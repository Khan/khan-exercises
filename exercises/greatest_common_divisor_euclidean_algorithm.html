When you are finding the greatest common divisor of A and B using the Euclidean algorithm, which one of the choices is a sub-problem you would end up solving as you go through the Euclidean algorithm?

Another way to express the problem of finding the greatest common divisor of A and B is:

\gcd(A, B) = {?}

\gcd(SMALLER, REMAINDER)

  • \gcd(A, B * 2)
  • \gcd(B, REMAINDER)
  • \gcd(A * 2, B)

The greatest common divisor is the largest number that is a factor (or divisor) of both A and B. Euclid's algorithm finds the greatest common divisor by following these steps:

1) Find the remainder that you get after you divide the bigger number by the smaller one.
2) Repeat the previous step with the remainder and the smaller number until the remainder is zero.
3) When the remainder is zero, the smaller number is the greatest common divisor.

The remainder of GREATER / SMALLER is 0, so the algorithm terminates and the smaller number SMALLER is the greatest common divisor.

The remainder of GREATER / SMALLER is REMAINDER. So the next sub-problem the algorithm has to solve is \gcd(SMALLER, REMAINDER).
