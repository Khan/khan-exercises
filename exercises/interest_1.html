INVESTMENT

The equation for simple interest is:

A = P (1 + r)^t

Where P is the principal (amount invested), r is the interest rate, t is the number of years and A is the final amount

The equation can be re-arranged to get:

P = \displaystyle\frac{A}{(1 + r)^t}

Percent means "out of one hundred," so INTEREST\% is equivalent to \displaystyle\frac{INTEREST}{100} which is equivalent to INTEREST / 100.

Substituting the values in to the equation gives:

P = \displaystyle\frac{$GOAL}{(1 + INTEREST / 100)^TIME}

TOTAL_PRICE

The amount of interest paid each year is equal to the cost of the VEHICLE multiplied by the interest rate.

Annual interest paid = $DOLLARS \times INTEREST\%

Percent means "out of one hundred," so INTEREST\% is equivalent to \displaystyle\frac{INTEREST}{100} which is equivalent to INTEREST / 100.

Annual interest paid = $DOLLARS \times INTEREST / 100 = $(INTEREST / 100 * DOLLARS).toFixed(2)

The total interest paid is the annual interest multiplied by the number of years.

Total interest paid = $DOLLARS \times INTEREST / 100 \times TIME = $(INTEREST / 100 * DOLLARS * TIME).toFixed(2)

Add the cost of the item to the interest paid to get the total cost.

Total cost = $DOLLARS \times INTEREST / 100 \times TIME + $DOLLARS
