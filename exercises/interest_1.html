person(1) borrows $commafy(PRINCIPAL) today and must repay $commafy(FUTURE_VALUE) exactly 1 year from now. What is the rate of interest person(1) will have to pay on the loan?

INTEREST_RATE

The interest rate paid on the loan can be determined by dividing the amount of interest paid on the loan by the original amount that person(1) borrowed.

Expressed as a formula, this is:
r = (Total Interest Paid / Principal Amount) * 100\%

Substituting our values:
r = ((FUTURE_VALUE - PRINCIPAL) / PRINCIPAL) * 100\%
r = INTEREST_RATE\%

person(1) would like to put aside some money in a bank to buy a new car 1 year from now. The car costs $commafy(FUTURE_VALUE). If the bank will pay person(1) an interest rate of INTEREST_RATE\% per year on his(1) money, how much money does he(1) need to put down today in order to have enough to purchase the car in 1 year?

PRINCIPAL

The final amount is the sum of the principal amount person(1) invested and the interest that the bank paid to him(1).

Mathematically we can express this as:
Final Amount = Principal + Principal * r

Substituting our values: FUTURE_VALUE = Principal + Principal * INTEREST_RATE/100

Substituting x for Principal, we have an equation with one unknown:
FUTURE_VALUE = x + x * INTEREST_RATE/100
x = $commafy(PRINCIPAL)

person(1) wants to put $commafy(PRINCIPAL) into a savings account, which pays a compound annual interest of INTEREST_RATE \% How long will it approximately take for person(1) to double his(1) money?

DOUBLING_ESTIMATE

We can use the Rule of 72 as a handy estimate for how long it will take person(1) to double his(1) money.

Doubling Time = \frac{72}{r}

Doubling Time = \frac{72}{INTEREST_RATE} = DOUBLING_ESTIMATE years
