Does 16 fit inside INTEGER? In other words, is it less than INTEGER?

Because 16 fits inside INTEGER, put a 1 in the 16s-place and subtract 16 from INTEGER. [Explain this]
Because 16 does not fit inside INTEGER, we put a 0 in the 16s-place and move on to the next lowest place value. [Explain this]
The number in each place value indicates how many times we should add that value to get to the total number. For example, putting a 4 in the 100s place in Base-10 says we have 4 hundreds. Similarly, in binary putting a 1 in the 16s place would indicate that we have 1 sixteen that fits inside of INTEGER. Placing a 0 in the 16s place would indicate that sixteen does not fit inside INTEGER.

Because 8 fits inside INTEGER % 16, we put a 1 in the 8s-place and subtract 8 from what is left.
