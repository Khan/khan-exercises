Determine where f(x) intersects the x-axis.

SQUAREx^2 + LINEARx + CONSTANT

x=A, and x=B

  • x=-A, and x=-B
  • x=B, and x=LINEAR/SQUARE
  • x=SQUARE, and x=LINEAR

The function interesects the x-axis where f(x)=0 so you need to solve the equation:

SQUAREx^2 + LINEARx + CONSTANT=0

Dividing both sides by SQUARE gives:

x^2 + SIMPLELINEARx + SIMPLECONSTANT=0

The coefficient on the x term is SIMPLELINEAR and the constant term is SIMPLECONSTANT, so we need to find two numbers that add up to SIMPLELINEAR and whose product is SIMPLECONSTANT

This works for the numbers -A and -B:

(-A)+(-B)=SIMPLELINEAR

-A*-B=SIMPLECONSTANT

So:

(x+-A)(x+-B)=0

x=A and x=B
